Camel

Interesting facts about camel

  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can’t eat.
  • Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms.

chameleon

Interesting facts about chameleon

  • their feet work like salad tongs.
  • almost half of all known species live in madagascar
  • chameleon vary wildly in size.

cicada

Interesting facts about cicada

  • cicada are physically distinguished by their stout bodies, broad heads, clear-membraned wings, and large compound eyes.
  • there are more than 3000 species of cicada.
  • periodical cicadas occur most years in different geographic regions

panda

Interesting facts about panda

  • Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim.
  • Pandas go from pink to white and black (or brown).
  • An adult panda can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day!

pigeon

Interesting facts about pigeon

  • Pigeons are highly sociable animals. They will often be seen in flocks of 20-30 birds.
  • Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities. They can detect sounds at far lower frequencies than humans are able to, and can thus hear distant storms and volcanoes.
  • Pigeons and humans have lived in close proximity for thousands of years.

teddy-bear

Interesting facts about teddy bear

  • The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906.
  • The term bear-hug was first recorded in 1846.
  • In 1902 in Germany, Steiff launched “Bear 55 PB”, the first toy bear with jointed arms and legs.
